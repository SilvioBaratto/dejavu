MASTER TECHNOLOGY SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Technology Services Agreement (this "Agreement"), effective as of January 15, 2024
(the "Effective Date"), is entered into by and between:

VertexCore Systems, Inc., a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the State of
Delaware, having its principal place of business at 1200 Innovation Drive, Suite 800, Wilmington,
Delaware 19801 ("Provider"); and

Novalis Financial Group LLC, a limited liability company organized and existing under the laws of
the State of New York, having its principal place of business at 350 Park Avenue, 25th Floor, New
York, New York 10022 ("Client").

Provider and Client are referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the
"Parties."

ARTICLE 2 — SCOPE OF SERVICES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.1  Managed Infrastructure Services.  Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement,
     Provider shall provide to Client the following categories of managed services during the
     Term:

     (a) Infrastructure Management.  Provider shall design, deploy, configure, monitor, maintain,
         and manage the infrastructure components required to support Client's technology
         operations, including:

         (i)   Server and compute infrastructure, including both on-premises equipment at
               Client's designated data centers and cloud-based virtual infrastructure on
               Amazon Web Services and Microsoft Azure;

         (ii)  Storage systems, including primary, secondary, and archival storage with tiered
               access and automated data lifecycle management;

         (iii) Networking equipment and software-defined networking, including firewalls,
               intrusion detection and prevention systems, load balancers, VPN gateways, and
               wide-area networking connecting Client's offices in New York, Chicago, San
               Francisco, and London;

         (iv)  Backup and recovery infrastructure, including daily incremental and weekly full
               backups of all Client Data; and

         (v)   End-user computing infrastructure management for Client's employee workstations,
               laptops, mobile devices, and associated software.

     (b) Security Operations.  Provider shall operate a 24×7×365 Security Operations Center
         (the "SOC") dedicated to monitoring Client's security posture and responding to Cyber
         Incidents. The SOC services shall include:

         (i)   Continuous monitoring of security information and event management (SIEM) systems
               for anomalous activity;

         (ii)  Vulnerability scanning and penetration testing performed no less frequently than
               quarterly;

         (iii) Patch management, including applying critical security patches within forty-eight
               (48) hours of release and non-critical patches within thirty (30) days;

         (iv)  Identity and access management, including privileged access management,
               multi-factor authentication enforcement, and quarterly access reviews;

         (v)   Threat intelligence subscription and integration; and

         (vi)  Incident response activities in accordance with the Incident Response Plan.

     (c) Application Management and Support.  Provider shall manage and support the following
         Client-designated applications: (i) Portfolio Management System (Aladdin by BlackRock);
         (ii) Order Management System (FlexTrade); (iii) Risk Management Platform (Numerix);
         (iv) Client Reporting Platform (Addepar); (v) Compliance Monitoring System
         (StarCompliance); and (vi) such additional applications as the Parties may agree in
         writing from time to time.  Application management services shall include monitoring,
         performance tuning, patch and release management, incident triage and resolution, and
         coordination with third-party software vendors on Client's behalf.

     (d) Data Management and Analytics.  Provider shall provide the following data management
         services: (i) operation of Client's data warehouse and data lake infrastructure;
         (ii) data integration pipeline development and maintenance, connecting market data
         feeds, custodian data, counterparty data, and internal operational data; (iii) data
         quality monitoring and remediation; (iv) development and maintenance of reporting and
         analytics dashboards; and (v) regulatory reporting data preparation support.

     (e) Cloud Optimization Services.  Provider shall perform ongoing cloud cost optimization
         activities, including rightsizing of cloud instances, identification and elimination of
         unused resources, reserved instance and savings plan procurement on Client's behalf, and
         preparation of monthly cloud spend reports comparing actual spend against budget.

2.2  Implementation Services.  Provider shall perform the one-time Implementation Services
     described in Schedule A, Section A-2, including data migration, system integration, user
     training, and go-live support.  The Implementation Services shall be completed within one
     hundred twenty (120) days of the Effective Date, subject to Client's timely provision of
     required information, access, and cooperation.

2.3  Professional Services.  From time to time during the Term, the Parties may execute
     Statements of Work for Professional Services.  Each Statement of Work shall specify the
     scope of services, Deliverables, timeline, fees, and acceptance criteria for the applicable
     engagement.  Provider shall not be obligated to commence any Professional Services under a
     Statement of Work until such Statement of Work has been executed by authorized
     representatives of both Parties.

2.4  Change Control.  Any request for a material change to the scope, specifications, or timeline
     of the Services shall be processed through the Change Control Process described in Schedule
     A, Section A-4.  Provider shall not be obligated to implement any change that has not been
     authorized through the Change Control Process, and Client shall not be obligated to pay for
     any out-of-scope work performed without prior written authorization.

2.5  Client Obligations.  Client shall:

     (a) Designate a Client relationship manager as a primary point of contact for Provider;

     (b) Provide Provider with reasonable access to Client's facilities, systems, and personnel
         as necessary for Provider to perform the Services;

     (c) Respond to Provider's requests for approvals, information, and decisions within five
         (5) Business Days;

     (d) Ensure that Client's systems and environments that interface with Provider's Systems
         meet the technical requirements set forth in Schedule A;

     (e) Comply with Provider's reasonable security policies and access control requirements;

     (f) Promptly notify Provider of any material changes to Client's regulatory requirements
         or compliance obligations; and

     (g) Maintain the software licenses and third-party service agreements necessary for
         Client's systems that Provider supports.

2.6  Subcontracting.  Provider may engage Subcontractors to perform portions of the Services,
     provided that:

     (a) Provider shall remain primarily responsible to Client for the performance of all
         obligations under this Agreement, including Services performed by Subcontractors;

     (b) Provider shall not engage any Subcontractor to perform services involving access to
         Sensitive Financial Data without Client's prior written consent; and

     (c) All Subcontractors who access Client Data shall be bound by confidentiality and data
         security obligations no less restrictive than those set forth in this Agreement.

ARTICLE 3 — FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1  Monthly Fee.  Client shall pay Provider a fixed monthly fee of Eighty-Five Thousand Dollars
     ($85,000.00) (the "Monthly Fee") for the Managed Infrastructure Services described in
     Section 2.1.  The Monthly Fee is payable in advance on the first (1st) Business Day of each
     calendar month.  For any partial month at the commencement or termination of this Agreement,
     the Monthly Fee shall be prorated on a per diem basis.

3.2  Implementation Services Fee.  Client shall pay Provider a one-time fee of Three Hundred
     Thousand Dollars ($300,000.00) for the Implementation Services described in Section 2.2,
     payable in three (3) equal installments of One Hundred Thousand Dollars ($100,000.00) each:

     (a) The first installment on the Effective Date;
     (b) The second installment upon completion of data migration; and
     (c) The third installment upon formal acceptance of all Deliverables in Schedule A,
         Section A-2.

3.3  Professional Services Fees.  Fees for Professional Services shall be as set forth in the
     applicable Statement of Work.  Unless otherwise specified in a Statement of Work,
     Professional Services shall be invoiced monthly based on actual hours worked at the rates
     set forth in Schedule B.

3.4  Expenses.  Client shall reimburse Provider for all reasonable and documented out-of-pocket
     travel, accommodation, and related expenses incurred by Provider's personnel in connection
     with on-site services performed at Client's request, provided that: (a) such expenses exceed
     Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00) in any single month; and (b) Provider obtains Client's prior
     written approval for any single expense exceeding Two Thousand Five Hundred Dollars
     ($2,500.00).  Provider shall submit expense reports with original receipts within thirty (30)
     days of incurring the expense.

3.5  Annual Fee Adjustment.  Beginning on January 15, 2025, and on each anniversary of the
     Effective Date thereafter during the Term, the Monthly Fee shall be adjusted by the lesser
     of: (a) three percent (3%); or (b) the percentage change in the U.S. Consumer Price Index
     for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U) for the preceding twelve (12) months, as published by the
     U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics.  Provider shall provide Client with thirty (30) days' prior
     written notice of any such adjustment.

3.6  Invoicing.  Provider shall issue invoices to Client via electronic mail to the billing
     contact designated by Client.  Invoices for Monthly Fees shall be issued no later than the
     fifth (5th) Business Day of each month for services to be rendered in that month.  Invoices
     for Professional Services shall be issued monthly in arrears, no later than the fifth (5th)
     Business Day of the following month.

3.7  Payment Terms.  Client shall pay all undisputed invoices within thirty (30) days of receipt
     (the "Payment Due Date").  Payment shall be made by wire transfer to the bank account
     designated by Provider in writing.  All amounts payable under this Agreement are stated in
     United States Dollars, and Client shall pay in United States Dollars.

3.8  Late Payment.  If Client fails to pay any undisputed amount by the Payment Due Date, such
     overdue amount shall accrue interest at the rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per
     month, or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is less, from the Payment
     Due Date until the date of actual payment.  Provider shall notify Client in writing of any
     overdue payment, and if such payment remains outstanding for thirty (30) days after such
     notice, Provider may, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity,
     suspend performance of the Services upon ten (10) Business Days' prior written notice to
     Client.

3.9  Disputed Invoices.  Client may dispute any invoice or portion thereof in good faith by
     providing Provider with written notice specifying in reasonable detail the amount disputed
     and the basis for the dispute within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice.
     Client shall pay all undisputed portions of any invoice by the Payment Due Date.  The
     Parties shall negotiate in good faith to resolve disputed amounts within thirty (30) days of
     Provider's receipt of the dispute notice.

3.10 Taxes.  All fees and other amounts payable by Client under this Agreement are exclusive of
     applicable federal, state, local, and foreign sales, use, excise, value-added, and other
     taxes (collectively, "Taxes").  Client shall be responsible for and shall pay all Taxes
     applicable to the Services, except for taxes based on Provider's net income, franchise
     taxes, and employment taxes imposed on Provider's personnel.  If Client is required to
     withhold any Taxes from any payments under this Agreement, Client shall gross up such
     payments so that Provider receives the full amount due.

3.11 Benchmarking.  Not more than once every two (2) years, Client may engage an independent
     third-party benchmarking firm to compare the pricing for the Services against market rates
     for comparable services from comparable providers.  If the benchmarking study concludes that
     the fees payable under this Agreement exceed market rates by more than fifteen percent (15%),
     the Parties shall negotiate in good faith for sixty (60) days to agree on adjusted pricing.
     If the Parties are unable to agree, Client may provide notice of termination under Section
     5.3, subject to the notice periods set forth therein.

ARTICLE 4 — TERM AND RENEWAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4.1  Initial Term.  This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier
     terminated as provided herein, shall continue for a period of three (3) years ending on
     January 14, 2027 (the "Initial Term").

4.2  Renewal Term.  This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods
     (each, a "Renewal Term") upon the expiration of the Initial Term or any then-current Renewal
     Term, unless either Party provides the other Party with written notice of non-renewal at
     least one hundred eighty (180) days prior to the expiration of the then-current Term.

4.3  Effect of Expiration.  Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

     (a) All outstanding Statements of Work shall automatically terminate, unless the Parties
         agree in writing to continue a specific Statement of Work on a separate, stand-alone
         basis;

     (b) Each Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the other Party's election) all
         Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession, except to the extent
         retention is required by Applicable Law or necessary for the Party's legal compliance
         purposes; and

     (c) All payment obligations accrued prior to expiration or termination shall survive.

4.4  Transition Assistance.  Upon the expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason,
     Provider shall provide transition assistance services to Client for a period of up to one
     hundred eighty (180) days (the "Transition Period"), at Client's request, to assist Client
     in migrating the Services to an alternative provider or in-house operation.  During the
     Transition Period, Provider shall:

     (a) Continue to perform the Services at the then-current fee rates;
     (b) Cooperate in good faith with Client and any replacement provider;
     (c) Provide Client with copies of all Client Data in Provider's possession in mutually
         agreed formats;
     (d) Provide reasonable documentation and knowledge transfer to the replacement provider;
         and
     (e) Make appropriate Provider personnel available for knowledge transfer sessions.

ARTICLE 5 — TERMINATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5.1  Termination for Cause by Client.  Client may terminate this Agreement for cause upon thirty
     (30) days' prior written notice to Provider if any of the following events (each, a
     "Termination Trigger") occurs, provided that the applicable cure period set forth in this
     Section has expired without cure:

     (a) Material Breach.  Provider commits a material breach of any of its representations,
         warranties, or obligations under this Agreement, and fails to cure such breach within
         thirty (30) days after receipt of Client's written notice specifying the breach in
         reasonable detail; provided, however, that if such breach is of a nature that cannot
         reasonably be cured within thirty (30) days, Provider shall not be in default if it
         commences cure within thirty (30) days and diligently pursues such cure to completion
         within ninety (90) days of the original notice.

     (b) Repeated Service Level Failures.  Provider fails to meet the Service Level Targets set
         forth in Schedule C in any three (3) calendar months within any rolling twelve (12)
         month period, and such failures are not attributable to Force Majeure Events, Client's
         actions or omissions, or the actions of third parties beyond Provider's reasonable
         control.  For this Termination Trigger, no cure period applies; Client may exercise
         its termination right upon the occurrence of the third qualifying month.

     (c) Security Breach.  Provider suffers a Cyber Incident attributable to Provider's
         negligence or willful misconduct that results in unauthorized access to, disclosure of,
         or destruction of Sensitive Financial Data, and Provider fails to remediate the root
         cause of such Cyber Incident within sixty (60) days of Client's written notice.

     (d) Insolvency.  Provider: (i) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the
         benefit of creditors, or is unable to pay its debts as they become due; (ii) has a
         receiver, trustee, or liquidator appointed for it or a substantial portion of its
         assets; (iii) commences, or has commenced against it, any bankruptcy, reorganization,
         arrangement, insolvency, dissolution, liquidation, or similar proceeding under the laws
         of the United States or any state thereof; or (iv) ceases to conduct business in the
         ordinary course; provided that, in the case of an involuntary proceeding, Client's
         right to terminate shall not arise until such proceeding has not been dismissed within
         sixty (60) days of commencement.  No cure period applies to this Termination Trigger.

     (e) Change of Control.  Provider undergoes a Change of Control (as defined below) to a
         Provider Competitor (as defined in Schedule A), and Provider fails to provide Client
         with reasonable assurances, within sixty (60) days of Client's written request, that
         the acquirer will assume all of Provider's obligations under this Agreement without
         material modification.  "Change of Control" means: (i) the sale or transfer of more
         than fifty percent (50%) of the outstanding voting securities of Provider; (ii) any
         merger, consolidation, or reorganization of Provider in which the holders of Provider's
         outstanding voting securities immediately prior to such transaction hold less than fifty
         percent (50%) of the voting power of the surviving or successor entity; or (iii) the
         sale of all or substantially all of Provider's assets.

5.2  Termination for Cause by Provider.  Provider may terminate this Agreement for cause upon
     thirty (30) days' prior written notice to Client if:

     (a) Client fails to pay any undisputed amount due under this Agreement within thirty (30)
         days after receipt of Provider's written notice of non-payment (thirty-day cure period);

     (b) Client commits a material breach of its confidentiality obligations under Article 7
         and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice (thirty-day cure period);

     (c) Client commits a material breach of its Intellectual Property obligations under
         Article 8 and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of written notice (thirty-day cure
         period); or

     (d) Client becomes insolvent or undergoes insolvency proceedings as described in the
         analogous provisions of Section 5.1(d) (no cure period).

5.3  Termination for Convenience by Client.  Client may terminate this Agreement for convenience
     (i.e., without cause) upon one hundred eighty (180) days' prior written notice to Provider.
     In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Provider a
     termination fee equal to three (3) months of the Monthly Fee then in effect, in addition to
     all fees for Services rendered through the termination date and all committed expenses
     incurred by Provider in reliance on this Agreement.

5.4  Termination for Convenience by Provider.  Provider may terminate this Agreement for
     convenience (i.e., without cause) upon three hundred sixty-five (365) days' prior written
     notice to Client.  Such notice shall be irrevocable, and Provider shall continue to perform
     all obligations under this Agreement during the notice period without degradation of service
     levels.

5.5  Effect of Termination.  Upon termination of this Agreement:

     (a) All licenses granted by Provider to Client under this Agreement shall immediately
         terminate, except for licenses necessary for Client to use Deliverables already
         delivered and accepted;

     (b) Each Party shall immediately cease using the other Party's Confidential Information,
         except as necessary for the orderly wind-down of the relationship;

     (c) Provider shall promptly deliver to Client all Client Data in Provider's possession in
         formats mutually agreed by the Parties; and

     (d) The rights and obligations set forth in Articles 7, 8, 10, 11, 12, and 14, and in
         Sections 3.10, 4.3, 4.4, 5.5, and 5.6, shall survive termination or expiration of
         this Agreement.

5.6  Return of Data.  Provider shall return all Client Data to Client within fifteen (15)
     Business Days of the effective date of termination.  Provider shall retain no copies of
     Client Data after completing the return, except as required by Applicable Law.  Provider
     shall provide Client with a written certification confirming the return and deletion of
     all Client Data within five (5) Business Days after completing such return and deletion.

SCHEDULE A — DESCRIPTION OF SERVICES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A-1.  Managed Infrastructure Services Scope.

A-1.1 Infrastructure Management.

      Provider shall manage Client's technology infrastructure across the following environments:

      Primary Production Environment:
      - Data Center: Equinix NY5, Secaucus, New Jersey
      - Cloud: AWS us-east-1 (primary) and AWS us-west-2 (disaster recovery standby)
      - Servers: 48 physical servers (24 primary compute nodes, 12 database nodes,
        12 storage nodes) plus up to 200 cloud virtual machines (auto-scaled)
      - Storage: 500 TB primary SSD storage, 1 PB spinning-disk archival storage,
        AWS S3 Glacier for seven-year archival retention
      - Network: Redundant 10 Gbps data center interconnects, 100 Gbps core backbone,
        BGP multi-homed internet access with two upstream providers

      Secondary / Disaster Recovery Environment:
      - Data Center: Equinix CH2, Chicago, Illinois
      - Cloud: AWS us-east-2 (secondary disaster-recovery)
      - Configuration: Warm standby with automated failover capability within the
        Recovery Time Objective of four (4) hours

      Office WAN Connectivity:
      - New York headquarters: Dual 1 Gbps MPLS circuits, LTE cellular backup
      - Chicago office: 500 Mbps MPLS circuit, cable broadband backup
      - San Francisco office: 500 Mbps MPLS circuit, cable broadband backup
      - London office: 500 Mbps MPLS circuit, VSAT satellite backup

A-1.2 Monitoring and Alerting.

      Provider shall maintain 24×7×365 monitoring from its network operations center (NOC)
      covering all infrastructure components:

      - CPU utilization (alert threshold: sustained >85% for 10 minutes)
      - Memory utilization (alert threshold: sustained >90% for 10 minutes)
      - Disk utilization (alert threshold: >80% capacity)
      - Network bandwidth utilization (alert threshold: sustained >70% of circuit capacity)
      - Application response times (alert threshold: >2× baseline for 5 minutes)
      - Database query performance (alert threshold: query p95 latency >3× baseline)
      - Backup job status (alert on failure within 30 minutes of completion window)
      - Security events (continuous, real-time via SIEM)

      All alerts shall be triaged and escalated in accordance with the severity matrix in
      Schedule C, Section C-3.

A-1.3 Capacity Planning.

      Provider shall conduct quarterly capacity reviews and provide Client with a written
      capacity report within ten (10) Business Days after the end of each calendar quarter.
      The capacity report shall include: current utilization trends, projected growth
      requirements for the next twelve (12) months, recommended capacity additions, and
      associated cost estimates.

A-2.  Implementation Services.

A-2.1 Implementation Phases.

      Phase 1 — Discovery and Design (Weeks 1–4):
      Provider shall conduct an assessment of Client's existing technology environment,
      interview key stakeholders, document as-is architecture, and produce a detailed design
      document for the target-state architecture and migration approach.  Deliverables include:
      As-Is Architecture Assessment Report, Target-State Architecture Design Document, and

SCHEDULE C — SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

C-1.  Definitions.

      "Availability" means the percentage of time during a calendar month that the Production
      Systems are accessible and functional, calculated as:
          [(Total Minutes in Month − Downtime Minutes) / Total Minutes in Month] × 100%.

      "Downtime" means any period during which the Production Systems are unavailable due
      to a service outage, excluding: (a) Scheduled Maintenance Windows; (b) Emergency
      Maintenance; (c) Force Majeure Events; (d) outages caused by Client's actions or
      omissions; and (e) outages caused by third-party services not under Provider's control.

      "Critical Incident" means an outage or degradation that renders the Production Systems
      completely inaccessible or causes complete unavailability of a Core Application, with
      no available workaround.

      "High Incident" means an outage or degradation that significantly impairs a Core
      Application's functionality, with a workaround available.

      "Medium Incident" means a partial service degradation that does not prevent use of Core
      Application functionality.

      "Low Incident" means a minor issue with minimal impact on service delivery.

SCHEDULE D — DATA SECURITY REQUIREMENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

D-1.  Data Classification and Handling.

      Provider shall classify Client Data into the following categories and apply the controls
      described below to each category:

      Category 1 — Sensitive Financial Data:
      - Includes: MNPI, NPPI under Gramm-Leach-Bliley, Reg S-P data, Client-designated SFD
      - Storage:  AES-256 encryption at rest; HSM-managed keys; key rotation ≤ 365 days
      - Transit:  TLS 1.3 minimum; no unencrypted transmission permitted
      - Access:   Privileged Access Management (PAM) required; full immutable audit log;
                  just-in-time access provisioning; two-person authorization for bulk export
      - Backup:   Encrypted with separate key management instance; keys stored off-site

      Category 2 — Confidential Business Data:
      - Includes: Client Data not classified as Category 1
      - Storage:  AES-256 encryption at rest
      - Transit:  TLS 1.2 minimum
      - Access:   Role-based access control (RBAC); quarterly access recertification
      - Backup:   Encrypted backup

      Category 3 — Internal Operating Data:
      - Includes: Operational data used to deliver the Services, not attributable to Client
      - Controls: Standard enterprise information security controls

D-2.  Incident Response Timelines.

      The following response timelines apply to Cyber Incidents affecting Client Data:
      - Detection to containment: target ≤ 1 hour
      - Containment to initial Client notification: ≤ 24 hours from detection
      - Initial forensic assessment: ≤ 72 hours from detection
      - Full incident report to Client: ≤ 30 days from resolution
      - Law enforcement and regulatory reporting: as required by Applicable Law

D-3.  Audit Rights.

      Client shall have the right to audit Provider's information security controls no more
      than once per calendar year, upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice, during Business
      Hours and without material disruption to Provider's operations.  Provider may satisfy
      Client's audit rights by providing a current SOC 2 Type II report covering the period
      in question.  If Client elects to conduct its own audit or engage a third-party auditor:
      (a) Client bears the cost unless the audit reveals a material noncompliance;
      (b) Provider bears the cost if a material noncompliance is found; and
      (c) the third-party auditor shall be bound by a non-disclosure agreement acceptable
          to Provider.

D-4.  Subprocessor and Subcontractor Security.

      All Subcontractors who access Client Data shall:
      (a) Be bound by data security obligations no less restrictive than those in this
          Schedule D;
      (b) Undergo Provider's third-party risk assessment prior to engagement;
      (c) Provide Provider with their most recent SOC 2 Type II report upon request; and
      (d) Be listed in the approved Subcontractor register, which Provider shall maintain
          and provide to Client upon written request.

D-5.  Data Residency.

      All Client Data (Category 1 and Category 2) shall be stored and processed exclusively
      within the United States, in the data center facilities identified in Schedule A,
      Section A-1.1, unless Client provides prior written consent to a different location.
      Provider shall not transfer any Client Data to any jurisdiction outside the United
      States without Client's prior written consent and compliance with all applicable data
      transfer laws and regulations.
